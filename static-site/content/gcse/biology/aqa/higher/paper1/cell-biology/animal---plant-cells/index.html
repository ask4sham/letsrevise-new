Animal & Plant Cells

AQA GCSE Biology • Higher Tier • Cell Biology

🎯 Higher Tier Content 📚 Part of Cell Biology

📖 Definition

Animal cells have a nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, mitochondria, and ribosomes. Plant cells have all these plus a cell wall, chloroplasts, and a large vacuole. Higher tier students should understand the detailed molecular structures and mechanisms involved.

📝 Example Exam Question

Describe the differences between animal and plant cells. [2 marks]

✅ Model Answer

Animal cells have a nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, mitochondria, and ribosomes. Plant cells have all of these plus a cell wall for support, chloroplasts for photosynthesis, and a large permanent vacuole for storage. The cell wall is made of cellulose and provides structural support. Additionally, plant cells have plasmodesmata for cell communication and store starch in plastids.

💡 Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing plant and animal cell structures
  • Not labeling diagrams correctly
  • Forgetting the function of organelles
  • Not providing enough detail for higher mark questions
  • Failing to apply knowledge to new situations
