Cellular Respiration

How organisms release energy from food

💪 Energy Production 🔄 ATP Synthesis ⚡ Two Types

Cellular respiration is the process by which cells break down glucose to produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate), the energy currency of the cell. This happens in the mitochondria.

💨

Aerobic Respiration

Uses oxygen to produce maximum energy

Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon Dioxide + Water + Energy (ATP)

Key Features:

  • Uses oxygen (aerobic)
  • Produces 36-38 ATP molecules
  • Occurs in mitochondria
  • Main type in humans and animals
  • Produces CO₂ and H₂O as waste
⚡

Anaerobic Respiration

Occurs without oxygen, produces less energy

Glucose → Lactic Acid + Energy (2 ATP)

Key Features:

  • No oxygen required (anaerobic)
  • Produces only 2 ATP molecules
  • Occurs in cytoplasm
  • Used during intense exercise
  • Produces lactic acid (in animals)

🔬 The Powerhouse: Mitochondria

Mitochondria are often called the "powerhouses of the cell". They have a double membrane with inner folds called cristae that increase surface area for energy production.

📝 Aerobic Respiration: 3 Stages

1

Glycolysis

Glucose is broken down into pyruvate in the cytoplasm, producing 2 ATP molecules and 2 NADH.

2

Krebs Cycle

Pyruvate enters mitochondria and is broken down in a cycle, producing CO₂, ATP, NADH, and FADH₂.

3

Electron Transport Chain

NADH and FADH₂ donate electrons to produce most of the ATP (32-34 molecules) and water as byproduct.

⚡

ATP Production

Aerobic respiration produces 36-38 ATP per glucose molecule.

🔄

Energy Efficiency

About 40% of energy from glucose is converted to ATP (rest is heat).

🏃

Muscle Use

Muscles switch to anaerobic respiration during intense exercise, causing lactic acid buildup.

Aspect Aerobic Respiration Anaerobic Respiration
Oxygen Required ✅ Yes ❌ No
ATP Produced 36-38 molecules 2 molecules
Location Mitochondria Cytoplasm
Efficiency High Low
End Products CO₂ + H₂O Lactic acid (animals) or ethanol + CO₂ (plants/yeast)
🏃‍♂️

Exercise

Muscles use anaerobic respiration during sprinting, causing lactic acid buildup and muscle fatigue.

🍞

Baking

Yeast uses anaerobic respiration (fermentation) to produce CO₂, making bread rise.

🍺

Brewing

Yeast fermentation produces ethanol and CO₂ in beer and wine making.

💡 Did You Know?

An average human produces about 70 kg of ATP per day through cellular respiration, but only has about 50g stored at any time - meaning each ATP molecule is recycled 1400 times per day!
