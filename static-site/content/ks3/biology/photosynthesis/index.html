Photosynthesis

How plants make their own food using sunlight

☀️ Light Energy 🌿 Chlorophyll 🍃 Oxygen Production

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. This process is essential for life on Earth as it produces oxygen and food.

🌱 The Photosynthesis Process

Plants take in carbon dioxide (CO₂) and water (H₂O), use sunlight energy captured by chlorophyll, and produce glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) and oxygen (O₂).

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
🌬️

Inputs

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂): From the air
  • Water (H₂O): From the soil
  • Sunlight: Energy source
  • Chlorophyll: Green pigment
🌿

Outputs

  • Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆): Food for the plant
  • Oxygen (O₂): Released into air

🍃 Leaf Structure

Leaves are the main organs for photosynthesis:

  • Cuticle: Waxy layer prevents water loss
  • Upper Epidermis: Protective layer
  • Palisade Cells: Contain chloroplasts
  • Spongy Cells: Allow gas exchange
  • Stomata: Pores for gas exchange

🔬 Chloroplasts

Chloroplasts are where photosynthesis happens:

  • Thylakoids: Contain chlorophyll
  • Grana: Stacks of thylakoids
  • Stroma: Fluid where glucose is made
  • Chlorophyll: Green pigment absorbs light

📝 Process in Detail

1

Light Absorption

Chlorophyll in chloroplasts absorbs sunlight energy, mainly blue and red light (green is reflected).

2

Water Splitting

Water molecules are split into hydrogen and oxygen using light energy. Oxygen is released as a waste product.

3

Carbon Dioxide Fixation

Carbon dioxide from the air combines with hydrogen to form glucose, using energy from ATP.

4

Glucose Production

Glucose is produced and used by the plant for energy, growth, or stored as starch.

💡 Did You Know?

Photosynthesis produces approximately 130 terawatts of energy annually - that's about 6 times more than global human power consumption!
